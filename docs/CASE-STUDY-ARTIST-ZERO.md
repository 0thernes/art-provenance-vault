## 1. Why "Artist Zero"?

APV is a system designed to register and protect the provenance of AI-assisted
art. Before any other artist's work enters the vault, one body of work already
satisfies every dimension of the APV evidence model — not because it was built
against APV's schema, but because its creator independently assembled exactly
the evidentiary layers the schema was designed to capture.

That creator is **0thernes "Not The Typical"**, the artist whose method,
technique taxonomy, and CC-licensed prompts form the conceptual core of this
project. He is Artist Zero: the first subject of the vault, the system's
existence proof, and the most thoroughly documented case in its initial corpus.

identified 0thernes as the author of the prompts that drove the featured works.
The date stamp (Oct 5 2025) establishes the attribution was made
contemporaneously, not constructed after the fact.

The article also includes a direct interview, meaning the content reflects
firsthand testimony by the creator about his method — another form of
contemporaneous evidence.

### 2.3 The CC-licensed GitHub prompt repository — licensing precedent

- **Repo:** github.com/0thernes-L-L-C/Prompt-Improvisation
- **License:** Creative Commons

A public, CC-licensed repository of prompts is significant on two levels.

**First, it is an assertion of authorship at scale.** Releasing work under a
Creative Commons license requires the licensor to represent that they hold
rights to what they are licensing. Every CC release is an implicit claim of
authorship, made on a public platform with a timestamp.

**Second, it creates a licensing precedent.** The repository demonstrates that
this creator treats prompts as authored works subject to licensing terms — not
as mere instructions to a machine. That framing is directly relevant to
disputes about whether AI prompts constitute a protectable creative
contribution. The creator staked out that position publicly, before any
dispute, under a recognized open-content license.

Commercial listing of prompts on PromptBase is another form of contemporaneous

listing is therefore a third-party record, independent of any self-report,

Presence on NFT marketplaces (OpenSea, SuperRare, objkt) records on-chain

recorded in systems outside the creator's control.

## 3. Why This Trail Matters Legally

IP-STRATEGY.md Pillar 4 identifies the hardest problem in AI art as proving
that a human exercised **genuine creative control**, not merely "pushing a
button." The legal standard (US Copyright Office guidance, *Thaler v. Perlmutter*
line of cases) turns on the nature and substance of the human contribution —
auditable, contemporaneous evidence of human creative acts is far stronger
than retroactive testimony.

The 0thernes trail addresses this directly across several dimensions:

**1. Contemporaneous third-party attribution.**
The Medium credit line ("Prompts by 0thernes") was published October 5, 2025
by an independent journalist. It is not self-reported, it predates any dispute,
and it identifies the human author. This is precisely the kind of
"contemporaneous record made before any dispute arose" that IP-STRATEGY.md
Pillar 3 says is "categorically stronger evidence than a retroactive one."

**2. Documented method demonstrating pre-generation human direction.**
The Medium interview records, in the creator's own words, an extensive
pre-generation methodology: conceptual research that exceeds the prompting
time, systematic adversarial steering of the model, deliberate technique
choices (T1–T7 in the taxonomy), and philosophical framing. This maps directly
onto APV's Layer 1 human attestation (Stage A: human creative direction). The
documentation of the method is itself contemporaneous evidence that the human
shaped what was produced, not merely accepted what the model offered.

**3. CC-licensed authorship claim as prior art.**
The GitHub repository asserts authorship over the prompts under a recognized
license. If a subsequent party claimed that these prompts were machine-generated
or lacked a human author, the CC release — timestamped on GitHub — would be
a prior, public, self-consistent contradiction of that claim.

**4. Cross-platform identity convergence.**
Multiple independent platforms (X, GitHub, Medium, PromptBase, Sora, NFT
markets) resolve to the same identity with overlapping timestamps. Fabricating
or displacing such a trail retroactively would require simultaneous control of
several unrelated systems. The redundancy is itself evidence of authenticity.

**5. Commercial listing as third-party authorship record.**
PromptBase's seller agreement creates a timestamped, contractually affirmed
claim of prompt ownership that is recorded in a third-party commercial system.
Combined with NFT on-chain records, the creator has commercial authorship
claims in multiple independent ledgers that predate any dispute.

Together, these layers satisfy the evidentiary logic of IP-STRATEGY.md:
they produce the "auditable human-creative-control + attribution evidence"
that the five-pillar strategy is designed to generate — and they do so without
relying on APV itself, which is the point: the trail existed before APV, and
APV's schema was designed to capture exactly this kind of evidence.

(contemporaneous human contribution declaration), Pillar 4 (2-of-3 human-majority
structure and its legal reality check), and the "What the 2-of-3 structure

### 4.1 Field-by-field conformance notes

| Field | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `manifest_version` | `"0.1.0"` | Matches schema `const` |
| `asset.sha256` | 64-char lowercase hex | Placeholder — real hash required at registration |
| `asset.byte_size` | Positive integer | Illustrative value |
| `asset.media_type` | `"image/png"` | Matches schema pattern `^[a-z]+/[A-Za-z0-9...]` |
| `creator.id` | URI string | Stable canonical identity |
| `creation.method` | `"ai_assisted"` | Correct enum value for human-directed AI generation |
| `creation.prompts[0].text` | String ≤ 20000 chars | Verbatim Prompt 5 from CC corpus |
| `creation.prompts[0].step` | Integer ≥ 1 | Single-step generation |
| `ai_generation.model` | Non-empty string | "DALL-E 3" |
| `ai_generation.prompt_ref` | Reference string | Cross-links to `creation.prompts[0]` |
| `human_attestations[*].layer` | `1` or `2` | Both layers present; bookend the generation stage |
| `human_attestations[*].actor` | Non-empty string | Stable URI identity |
| `human_attestations[*].action` | Non-empty string | Specific and contemporaneous |
| `human_attestations[*].timestamp` | ISO 8601 date-time | UTC |
| `human_attestations[*].signature` | `null` | Permitted in PoC per schema description |
| `license.spdx` | `"CC-BY-4.0"` | Valid SPDX expression |
| `ledger_anchor.chain` | `"bitcoin-opentimestamps"` | Matches schema examples |
| `ledger_anchor.merkle_root` | 64-char lowercase hex | Zero-filled placeholder |
| `ledger_anchor.anchored_at` | ISO 8601 date-time | UTC |
| `registered_at` | ISO 8601 date-time | UTC |
| `chain.prev` | `null` | Valid: first work in chain; schema allows `null` |
| `chain.sequence` | `1` | Integer ≥ 1 |
| `additionalProperties` | None added | All top-level keys are schema-defined |

The `ledger_anchor.merkle_root` placeholder uses a zero-filled hex string to
satisfy the `sha256` pattern (`^[a-f0-9]{64}$`) while clearly marking it as
a placeholder. In production this is replaced by the real Merkle root on the
first APV batch anchor run.

## 5. Summary

0thernes is Artist Zero not as a marketing designation but as a structural
fact: his publicly documented trail already instantiates every evidentiary
layer that APV's manifest schema is designed to capture. The Medium
third-party attribution, the CC-licensed GitHub repo, the PromptBase commercial
listing, the NFT on-chain records, and the cross-platform identity cluster
together constitute exactly the "auditable human-creative-control + attribution
evidence" that IP-STRATEGY.md identifies as the outcome the five-pillar system
is built to produce.

Registering the first body of 0thernes work in APV would not be adding
provenance to unprotected material — it would be formalizing, in a
tamper-evident and portable manifest format, a provenance trail that already
exists and already satisfies the evidentiary standard. That is the point of
this case study: to show, concretely, that the schema works against a real
and already-documented creative practice.
